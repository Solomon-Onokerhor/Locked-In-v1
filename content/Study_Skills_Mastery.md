# Master "Locking In": The Science of High-Intensity Study 🏔️🧠

To succeed as a student, it's not about how *long* you study, but how *deep* you focus. This guide explains the core principles behind the **Locked In** philosophy.

## 1. Active Recall: Store, Don't Scan
Rereading notes is the biggest waste of time. Your brain only learns when it is forced to *retrieve* information.
- **The Method**: After reading a page, close the book and write down everything you remember.
- **The "Locked In" Way**: Use the Chat in your room to explain concepts to others. Teaching is the ultimate form of active recall.

## 2. Spaced Repetition: Defeat the Forgetting Curve
Information fades within 24 hours unless it is refreshed.
- **The Schedule**: Review 1 day, 1 week, and 1 month after learning.
- **The "Locked In" Way**: Use your **Streak** to ensure you engage with at least one resource every single day. Even 5 minutes keeps the connections alive.

## 3. The 90-Minute Sprint
The human brain is optimized for 90-minute blocks of deep focus (Ultradian Rhythms).
- **The Method**: 90 minutes of zero-distraction work, followed by a 15-minute complete brain break (no phone!).
- **The "Locked In" Way**: Create rooms with a **Duration** of 90 minutes. It signals to others that this is a "Serious Hub."

## 4. Environment Design
Your physical space dictates your mental state.
- **Zero Friction**: Keep your study materials ready.
- **Digital Silence**: Put your phone in another room. Use **Locked In** on your desktop as your only open tab.
